Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD) surveillance relies on autopsy and neuropathologic evaluation.
The 1990-2000 CJD autopsy rate in California was 21%.
Most neurologists were comfortable diagnosing CJD (83%), but few pathologists felt comfortable diagnosing CJD (35%) or performing autopsy (29%).
Addressing obstacles to autopsy is necessary to improve CJD surveillance.
