A 49-year-old man developed ataxia, myoclonic jerks, cortical blindness, and dementia.
In 3 1/2 months, he rapidly deteriorated and died.
Clinical and autopsy diagnosis confirmed Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease.
The eyes were examined and bilateral optic atrophy was noted.
No other ocular changes were noted.
Optic atrophy had not been noted before death.
