The authors present an analysis of the clinical course of 6 cases of Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (patients were aged from 27 to 59 years).
The diagnosis was established during the life of the patients.
In the neurological status dementia and syndromes of pyramidal and extrapyramidal lesions predominated.
Neuropathological examinations in 5 cases demonstrated also considerable cerebellar damage, however, clinical signs of this damage were noted in two patients only.
EEG findings were of greatest importance among the laboratory investigations.
