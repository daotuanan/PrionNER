Serial magnetic resonance (MR) imagings of two autopsied patients with Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD) are presented.
Both patients showed a dramatic progression of brain atrophy.
The initial MR imagings were, however, interpreted as normal except for localized mild cortical atrophy in one patient.
When a normal MR image is obtained in a demented middle-aged or aged patient, CJD may still need to be ruled out: follow up MR imaging may be useful.
